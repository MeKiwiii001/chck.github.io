The Beginning of Something New

Good day, good night. Before the beginning of the first day of work immersion, we were assigned to work at the Municipal Development and Planning Office. The first thing that people did there was to greet us and they're so so heartwarming and so so kind talaga. Destine and I may be quiet but we enjoyed our moments there, especially during workshop or meetings. Why? Simple, there was free food. Like, food-food. Free snacks, lunch meals, after lunch delicacy, yummy.

But that's not the only reason why we enjoyed our stay in MPDO, we also enjoyed our work which was very engaging. Although, the work we did was not aligned to our future courses/dreams (Destine, Forensics. Me, IT/ComSci), we were delighted that they still asked us to do something that is VERY VERY important. Their stories and chismis did kept us awake most of the time but if we don't have anything to do or if we finished our assigned tasks, I scroll in socmed, play games, or sleep on my desk.

But to clear up something, we assisted in handing out foods during break time or lunch, greeting people, asking them to list their names and signatures for attendance, and assist them if they're struggling with something like helping out folks without computer/laptop knowledge regarding the use of Microsoft Office.

Before It All Started

My name is Rhay Meshach C. Budayao. I was born on Januray 9, 2008 at Baguio General Hospital (and yes, I'm 18 years old now which is very very scary). I partially lived my life as a kid at Brookside, Baguio, and I met many friends there, some have dysfuntional families and some have a normal, comforting household-- I was one of the dysfuntional families. I won't get into much details but I know how parents tend to argue from small things to big things-- I won't be like them.

Insert image URL in src=""

My mother may be strict when I was a child, but she's very loving and caring. And the reason why she was strict with me was not because to show her superiority and control over me but her act of love as a mother. My father on the other hand, was the chillest and also caring like my mother. He's also the reason why I love playing games.

He introduced me to Warcraft III: The Frozen Throne, which was remade in 2020 as Warcraft III: Reforged. It was a very popular PC game in the mid-2000s. At first, I didn't know what to do in the game, but he taught me how to play. I still remember introducing the game to my friends afterward, but they didn't know what to do either, they were just as clueless as I was when I first started. He also owned a PlayStation Portable (PSP) and we played Dissidia: Final Fantasy a lot-- it was one of my favorite games to play at the time.

"Life is a game. Until you realize it's pay to win."
